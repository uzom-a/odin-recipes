Nigerian Beans

Home

Description

This hearty Nigerian beans recipe is a staple dish made boiling brown or honey beans until soft, then cooking them in a delicious blend of onions, palm oil, and spices. Often enriched with crayfish, pepper, and sometimes fried plantains or yam on the side, it's a wholesome and filling meal. Nigerian beans is loved for its rich flavor, soft texture, and nutritional value, making it perfect for lunch or dinner.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups brown or honey beans
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons palm oil
  • Crayfish (ground)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1-2 seasoning cubes
  • Chopped fresh pepper (or ground dry pepper)
  • Optional: fried plantains or boiled yam

Steps

  1. Rinse and boil the beans until soft (about 45 minutes to 1 hour).
  2. Drain excess water if needed.
  3. In a separate pot, heat palm oil and saut√© onions and pepper.
  4. Add crayfish and seasoning cubes, then stir in the cooked beans.
  5. Let everything simmer together for 5 to 10 minutes.
  6. Adjust salt to taste and serve hot with plantains or yam.
